Configuration Tips
More availability = reduce k or t;
More confidentiality = increase m.
If someone controls t shards they can decrypt; with fewer than t shards they cannot.
m — in the context of RS is the number of redundant parity shards.
t — in the context of SSS, this is the threshold: the minimum number of Shamir shards required to recover the secret.
Choose whether restore should require detached archive approval.
Technical context
Split exports successor QVqcont-7 shards plus .archive-state.json, .cohort-binding.json, and .lifecycle-bundle.json. Without an external detached archive-approval signature over the archive-state descriptor, restore will block before the file is decrypted. Archive approval stays detached from shard creation: sign the exported .archive-state.json later, then use Attach to merge detached evidence into the lifecycle bundle without changing archive-state bytes.
