Configuration Tips
More availability = reduce k or t;
More confidentiality = increase m.
If someone controls t shards they can decrypt; with fewer than t shards they cannot.
m — in the context of RS is the number of redundant parity shards.
t — in the context of SSS, this is the threshold: the minimum number of Shamir shards required to recover the secret.
This Build flow currently emits the legacy manifest/bundle shard family and exports a signable .qvmanifest.json. Attach and Restore also support successor lifecycle archives, where archive-approval signatures target canonical archive-state bytes. Without an external detached signature attached later, restore will block and the file will not be decrypted.
